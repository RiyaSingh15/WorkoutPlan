Month 2 · Push / Pull / Legs ×2

Lift With Intent

Left knee: controlled tempo, moderate depth, stop on sharp pain — gentle loading helps rebuild strength. Rod (lower left leg): calf and shin-loading moves should start light; skip if you feel hardware irritation. Surgeon-cleared for weight training.

4-Week Progression

WeekWhat changes
W1FoundationSets as written · top of rep range · RPE 7 · rest as listed (90–120s on hip thrust/presses/hack squat) · cardio 10–15 min ×3
W2BuildOnce every set hits the top of its range, add ~5% load and reset to the bottom · +1 set on main lifts · RPE 7–8 · cardio 15–20 min ×3
W3PeakDrop set on lateral raises & leg extension · rest-pause on the last triceps move · heavier compounds · RPE 8–9 · hardest HIIT
W4Deload~2 sets · 60–70% load · RPE 5–6 · light cardio. Lets your leg and nervous system rebound.

Before Every Session

Warm-up

5–8 min: incline walk, leg swings, hip circles, glute bridges, one light set.

Tempo

~2 sec down, 1 sec up. Squeeze hard at the top.

Progress

Add weight or reps weekly with good form. Log it.

Cool-down

5 min: stretch what you trained, plus calf/hip-flexor.

The Week

Cardio

Steady-state

Incline fast walk, bike, or elliptical — 10–20 min after Days 1 & 2, optional 10 min after Days 4/5. Pace: could talk, wouldn't want to sing.

HIIT — Day 6

~12 min: 30s hard / 30s easy on bike, elliptical, or kettlebell swings — or 45s/45s incline-walk surges. Start at 5 rounds, build to 7–8.

No jump squats, burpees, box jumps, or running.
